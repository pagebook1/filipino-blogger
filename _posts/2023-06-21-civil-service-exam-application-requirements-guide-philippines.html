 The Civil Service Exam is a critical requirement for anyone who aspires to work in the public service sector in the Philippines. The Civil Service Commission (CSC) administers the exam twice a year, with the next one scheduled for August 20, 2023. If you're planning to take the exam, it's important to understand the civil service application requirements and qualifications. In this article, we provide a guide to help you prepare and file your application successfully.


Civil Service Application Requirements: When is the Next Exam?


The CSC has announced that the next Civil Service Exam will be held on August 20, 2023. The exam is open to the general public, including employees from different government agencies. Applicants can choose to take either the CSC-Professional or CSC-SubProfessional exam. The application period for both levels begins on May 22, 2023, and closes on June 21, 2023. Applicants are advised to file their applications early as the acceptance of applications is on a first-come, first-serve basis.


How to Apply for the Civil Service Exam in the Philippines?


To file your Civil Service Exam application, you must visit the CSC Regional Office (CSC RO) or any CSC RO's Field Office where you intend to take the exam. It's important to note that applicants must wear proper attire and avoid sleeveless shirts/blouses, shorts/short pants, and slippers.


Here are the requirements that you need to prepare before filing your application:


Accomplished Application Form (CS FORM No. 100 Revised September 2016) with signature and thumbprint spaces left blank â€“ to be accomplished in the presence of a CSC representative or processor during filing.


Four (4) colored Passport size photos taken within the last three months prior to application, complying with specific photo paper and background color requirements.


Valid identification card and photocopy of it. Refer to CSE List of Accepted IDs to check which ID is accepted.


Examination fee of Php 500.00, which is non-refundable.


For applicants whose ID does not contain their date of birth, submit a copy of their NSO or PSA Birth Certificate.


If an applicant has dual citizenship under RA 9225, submit a copy of a Certificate of Retention/ Re-acquisition of Philippine Citizenship issued by the Bureau of Immigration.


What are the Qualifications for the Civil Service Exam?


To qualify for the Civil Service Exam, applicants must meet the following requirements:


Be a Filipino citizen and at least 18 years old at the time of application.


Have good moral character and no criminal record or conviction for an offense involving moral turpitude.


NOT have been dishonorably discharged from the military, police, fire, jail service, or dismissed for cause from any civilian position in the government.


NOT have taken the same level of career service examination either through PPT or CSC Computerized Examination (CSC COMEX) within three (3) months from the last examination taken.


There is no educational attainment requirement to apply, but the applicant must meet the eligibility qualifications.


Exemptions for the Civil Service Exam


Applicants who are Bar and PRC Board passers are granted second-level civil service eligibility without taking the Civil Service Exam. Passers of Career Service Examination for Foreign Service Officer and individuals granted eligibility under special laws and CSC issuances may opt not to take the exam anymore.


Conclusion


Filing for the Civil Service Exam in the Philippines requires compliance with various civil service application requirements and eligibility qualifications. Preparing and submitting all the necessary documents before the deadline is crucial to a successful application. By passing the Civil Service Exam, applicants can acquire a government position and gain eligibility for promotions and career advancement. Whether you're filing for the CSC-Professional or CSC-Subprofessional exam, following the guidelines in this guide can help you get started on your journey to a career in public service.
