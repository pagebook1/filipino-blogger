If you're a STEM student preparing for a college entrance exam, you know how important a strong foundation in math is. Whether you're applying for engineering, science, or technology programs, you can expect a rigorous math test as part of the admission process. To help you get ready, we've curated 50+ math entrance exam questions and answers spanning algebra, geometry, calculus, and statistics.


These questions are designed to cover a range of difficulty levels, from basic concepts to more complex problem-solving scenarios. By practicing these questions, you'll not only build your confidence in solving math problems but also get a better understanding of the type of questions that could be on an entrance exam.


Here are some examples of the types of questions you can expect to find:

Algebra: Simplify expressions, solve equations, and find the slope and y-intercept of a line.

Geometry: Find the perimeter and area of 2D shapes, the volume of 3D shapes, and the equation of a line or circle.

Calculus: Find derivatives and indefinite integrals, solve limits, and find the area between curves.

Statistics: Calculate mean, median, mode, standard deviation, and correlation coefficients, and apply confidence intervals.

While these questions are not exhaustive of all the possible topics you might encounter in a math entrance exam, they provide a useful starting point for your preparation. By practicing regularly and seeking help with specific concepts that you find challenging, you can improve your math skills and increase your chances of success on your exam.


Remember, practicing with sample questions is just one part of the equation. You should also review core concepts, take practice exams, and get enough rest before your exam day. With a bit of dedication and effort, you can make sure you're well-prepared and confident as you take on the math section of your entrance exam.


In conclusion, check out our 50+ math entrance exam questions and answers for STEM students to get started on your preparation journey and take your first step towards your desired STEM program.



Algebra

Simplify the expression: (2x + 3)^2 - 5x.

Answer: 4x^2 + 10x + 9.


Solve for x: 2(x + 3) = 8.

Answer: x = 1.


Factor the expression: x^2 - 9.

Answer: (x + 3)(x - 3).


Solve the system of equations: 3x + 2y = 10 and 2x - y = 5.

Answer: x = 2, y = -1.


Find the slope and y-intercept of the line y = -2x + 5.

Answer: Slope = -2, y-intercept = 5.


Solve for x: 3x - 4 = 2(x + 1).

Answer: x = 2.


Factor the expression: 9x^2 - 16y^2.

Answer: (3x + 4y)(3x - 4y).


Simplify the expression: √(20) / √(5).

Answer: 2√(5).


Solve thesystem of equations: x + y = 4 and 2x - 3y = 7.

Answer: x = 5, y = -1.


Find the equation of the line that passes through the points (-1, 4) and (3, 8).

Answer: y = x + 5.


Geometry


Find the perimeter of a rectangle with length 8 cm and width 5 cm.

Answer: 26 cm.


Find the area of a parallelogram with base 7 cm and height 4 cm.

Answer: 28 cm^2.


Find the volume of a sphere with diameter 10 cm.

Answer: 524.15 cm^3.


Find the equation of the perpendicular bisector of the segment joining the points (1, 3) and (5, 7).

Answer: y = -x + 7.


Find the equation of the circle with center (2, 3) and radius 4.

Answer: (x - 2)^2 + (y - 3)^2 = 16.


Calculus 


Find the derivative of f(x) = 4x^3 - 2x^2 + 3.

Answer: f'(x) = 12x^2 - 4x.


Find the indefinite integral of f(x) = 3x^2 + 2x + 1.

Answer: ∫(3x^2 + 2x + 1) dx = x^3 + x^2 + x + C.


Find the limit of f(x) = (x^3 - 1) / (x - 1) as x approaches 1.

Answer: 3.


Find the area between the curves y = 4x^2 and y = 3x - 2.

Answer: 21/2.


Find the equation of the tangent line to the curve y = x^4 at the point (1, 1).

Answer: y = 4x - 3.


Statistics 


Calculate the mean, median, and mode of the data set: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10.

Answer: Mean = 6, median = 6, mode = none.


Calculate the standard deviation of the data set: 10, 12, 15, 18, 20.

Answer: 4.18.


Find the correlation coefficient (r) of the data set: x = 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and y = 3, 6, 9, 12, 15.

Answer: 1.


If the scores on an exam follow a normal distribution with a mean of 75 and a standard deviation of 8, what percentage of students scored above 85?

Answer: 6.68%.


A sample of size 20 has a mean of 25 and a standard deviation of 4. Construct a 95% confidence interval for the population mean.

Answer: (23.36, 26.64).


Algebra


Simplify the expression: (3x^2 - 2x + 4) - (x^2 - 5x + 6).

Answer: 2x^2 + 3x - 2.


Solve for x: 3(x - 2) = 5(x + 1).

Answer: x = 7.


Factor the expression: 4y^2 + 25z^2 - 20yz.

Answer: (2y - 5z)^2.


Simplify the expression: (5x - 2) / (3x + 1) + (x - 4) / (2x - 1).

Answer: (19x - 10) / (6x^2 + x - 2).


Solve the system of equations: 4x + 3y = 5 and 2x - y = 0.

Answer: x = 1, y = 2.


Geometry 


Find the perimeter of a square with side length 6 cm.

Answer: 24 cm.


Find the area of a trapezoid with bases 4 cm and 6 cm, and height 3 cm.

Answer: 15 cm^2.


Find the volume of a rectangular prism with length 10 cm, width 5 cm, and height 3 cm.

Answer: 150 cm^3.


Find the equation of the line that passes through the point (2, -1) and is perpendicular to the line y = 2x + 3.

Answer: y = (-1/2)x.


Find the equation of the circle with center (5, -2) and radius 2.

Answer: (x - 5)^2 + (y + 2)^2 = 4.


Calculus 


Find the derivative of f(x) = sin(x) - cos(x).

Answer: f'(x) = cos(x) + sin(x).


Find the indefinite integral of f(x) = 1 / (x^2 + 1).

Answer: ∫(1 / (x^2 + 1)) dx = arctan(x) + C.


Find the limit of f(x) = (x^2 - 4) / (x - 2) as x approaches 2.

Answer: 4.


Find the area between the curves y = 2x and y = x^2.

Answer: 8/3.


Find the equation of the normal line to the curve y = ln(x) at the point (1, 0).

Answer: y = -x + 1.


Statistics 


Calculate the mean, median, and mode of the data set: 5, 8, 12, 5, 6, 9, 12, 6.

Answer: Mean = 8, median = 7, mode = 5, 6, and 12.


Calculate the standard deviation of the data set: 20, 22, 25, 28, 30.

Answer: 3.42.


Find the correlation coefficient (r) of the data set: x = 3, 6, 9, 12, 15 and y = 2, 4, 6, 8, 10.

Answer: 1.


If the scores on an exam follow a normal distribution with a mean of 85 and a standard deviation of 10, what percentage of students scored between 75 and 95?

Answer: 68%.


A sample of size 30 has a mean of 50 and a standard deviation of 6. Construct a 99% confidence interval for the population mean.

Answer: (47.19, 52.81).


Algebra 


Simplify the expression: (2x^3 + 3x^2 - x) - (x^3 - 4x^2 + 2x - 1).

Answer: x^3 + 7x^2 - 3x + 1.


Solve for x: 2/x + 3/(x + 2) = 1/2.

Answer: x = -4 or x = 2.


Factor the expression: 3x^4 - 27x^2.

Answer: 3x^2(x + 3)(x - 3).


Simplify the expression: (4x - 5)(2x + 3) / (4x^2 - 9).

Answer: (2x - 1)(2x + 3) / (2x - 3)(2x + 3).


Solve the system of equations: 2x - y + 3z = 0, x + 2y - z = 1, and 3x + y + 2z = 2.

Answer: x = 1, y = 0, z = -1.
